Children & Young People

This space is just for you. Everything here is to help you stay safe, know your rights, and know who to call.

📌 What is Bullying?

Bullying is when someone keeps hurting you, leaving you out, or making you feel bad — on purpose. It is never your fault. Types include:

  • Physical — hitting, pushing, taking your belongings
  • Verbal — name-calling, threats, mockery
  • Social — spreading rumours, excluding you
  • Cyberbullying — hurtful messages or posts online

🔍 Signs You Are Being Bullied

  • You dread going to school every day
  • Your things keep going missing or getting damaged
  • You feel sad, angry or scared without knowing why
  • You eat alone or avoid spending time with others
  • You get hurt and try to make excuses for it

✅ What You Can Do

  • Tell a trusted adult — teacher, parent, or relative
  • Walk away calmly if you feel safe doing so
  • Write down what happened with the date and time
  • Screenshot cyberbullying — don't reply to messages
  • You are not alone — asking for help is strength

💬 You Have the Right to Feel Safe at School and at Home

Jamaica's Child Care and Protection Act says every child has the right to be protected from harm. Bullying is a form of harm. It is always okay to speak up.

⚠️ No Adult Has the Right to Hurt You

If any adult — including family members — hurts you physically, sexually, or emotionally, or ignores your basic needs, that is abuse. It is not normal. It is not love. It is not your fault.

🚫 Types of Child Abuse

  • Physical abuse: Hitting, burning, or kicking beyond any discipline
  • Sexual abuse: Any sexual touch or act by an adult or older person
  • Emotional abuse: Constantly belittled, humiliated, threatened, or ignored
  • Neglect: Not being given food, shelter, medical care, or love

💛 You Are Believed

Many children in Jamaica experience abuse and never tell anyone because they are afraid. But telling a trusted person is the bravest thing you can do. It can stop the abuse and keep you safe forever.

📞 Who to Tell

  • A teacher, school counsellor, or your principal
  • A trusted aunt, uncle, neighbour or friend's parent
  • Call 1-888-CHILDREN — free and confidential
  • Call 119 if you are in danger right now

🚨 Human Trafficking is a Crime — Even When it Seems Like Help

People who want to exploit children often pretend to be helpers, friends, or offer gifts and a "better life." Learning the warning signs can protect you.

🔴 Warning Signs

  • A stranger offers you gifts, money, or a "better life"
  • Someone asks you to travel alone with them
  • An adult wants to keep your friendship a secret
  • Someone takes photos or videos of you without permission
  • You are asked to do adult things in exchange for anything

🛡️ How to Stay Safe

  • Never accept rides from people you don't know well
  • Never meet someone online in secret without telling an adult
  • Always tell a trusted adult where you are going
  • If something feels wrong — it probably is. Trust yourself.
  • It is always okay to say NO to an adult.

⚖️ Under Jamaican Law, You Have the Right To:

  • Be protected from violence, abuse and neglect
  • Receive a quality education
  • Access healthcare and have your basic needs met
  • Be heard in decisions that affect your life
  • Be protected from sexual exploitation
  • Not be used for harmful or dangerous child labour

📜 Laws That Protect You

  • Child Care and Protection Act (2004)
  • Sexual Offences Act (2009)
  • Trafficking in Persons (Prevention) Act (2007)
  • UN Convention on the Rights of the Child
    (Jamaica ratified 1991)

🌟 These Are Not Just Words

These laws mean real people have a legal duty to protect you. If your rights are being violated, you can report it to the Office of the Children's Advocate at 876-929-8618 or visit oca.gov.jm.

Parents & Guardians

Knowledge, tools and resources to help you protect, nurture and advocate for your children every day.

🌱 Positive Parenting Practices

  • Show affection openly and consistently every day
  • Set clear, age-appropriate boundaries with explanation
  • Listen actively when your child speaks — do not dismiss
  • Use natural consequences instead of physical punishment
  • Praise effort and progress, not just results
  • Make time for regular one-on-one connection

🚫 Harmful Parenting Behaviours

  • Physical punishment (beating, slapping) — harmful to child development
  • Using fear, shame, or humiliation as discipline
  • Emotional unavailability or chronic neglect
  • Favouritism between children in the home
  • Exposing children to domestic violence
  • Using children as messengers in adult conflicts

💬 Building Open Communication

  • Create a "no judgment" space for your child's questions
  • Use age-appropriate language about body safety
  • Ask open questions: "How did that make you feel?"
  • Believe and validate your child when they share hard things
  • Never punish a child for telling the truth

📌 Free Parenting Support in Jamaica

The Child Protection and Family Services Agency (CPFSA) offers free parenting workshops and family support services across all parishes. Call 1-888-CHILDREN or visit cpfsa.gov.jm.

⚠️ If You Suspect Abuse, Act — You Don't Need to Be 100% Sure

Under the Child Care and Protection Act, suspicion alone is enough to make a report. Reporting is your legal and moral duty. You cannot be punished for a good-faith report.

🔴 Physical Signs

  • Unexplained bruises, burns, or cuts
  • Injuries that don't match the explanation given
  • Flinching at sudden movements or touch
  • Wearing long sleeves in hot weather to hide marks
  • Repeated "accidents" or unexplained hospital visits

🧠 Behavioural & Emotional Signs

  • Sudden withdrawal, aggression, or regression
  • Age-inappropriate sexual behaviour or knowledge
  • Intense fear of specific adults, places or situations
  • Dramatic changes in eating, sleep, or school performance
  • Nightmares, bedwetting, anxiety, or unexplained sadness

🏠 Neglect Warning Signs

  • Consistently hungry or quietly stealing food
  • Poor hygiene or inappropriate clothing for weather
  • Untreated medical, dental, or vision problems
  • Frequent school absences with no explanation
  • Parent appears chronically unavailable or impaired

🏫 Signs Your Child Is Being Bullied

  • Reluctance or fear of going to school each day
  • Coming home with damaged property or unexplained injuries
  • Mood changes — sadness, anger, withdrawal — after school
  • Withdrawing from activities and friends they used to love
  • Requesting extra money for "lunch" — possible extortion

👊 Signs Your Child May Be Bullying Others

  • Has money or new items they can't explain
  • Shows little empathy when others are hurt
  • Gets into fights and always blames others
  • Friends use aggression or humiliation as "fun"

✅ What You Should Do

  • Listen calmly — do not minimise or dismiss their experience
  • Document incidents with dates and details
  • Meet with the principal and guidance counsellor
  • If unresolved, escalate to the Ministry of Education
  • Consider counselling support for your child

🔴 Jamaica is a Source, Transit and Destination Country for Trafficking

Children are trafficked for sexual exploitation and forced labour. Girls are disproportionately targeted, and many are recruited by people they already know and trust.

⚠️ How Children Are Recruited

  • Promises of modelling, music, or entertainment careers
  • Romantic grooming by significantly older individuals
  • False promises of overseas work or education
  • Gifts of phones, clothes, shoes, or money to young girls
  • Social media recruitment and online grooming by strangers

🛡️ Protect Your Child

  • Know your child's online friends and activities
  • Talk openly about body safety, consent, and boundaries
  • Verify ALL job or travel opportunities independently
  • Keep communication open — make it safe to tell you anything
  • If something feels wrong about an adult's interest — act fast

⚖️ Your Legal Obligation as a Parent

Under the Child Care and Protection Act of Jamaica (2004), certain professionals (teachers, doctors, nurses, social workers) are legally mandated to report suspected child abuse. All Jamaicans are strongly encouraged to report. You cannot be prosecuted for making a good-faith report.

Village & Community Members

It takes a village to raise a child — and to protect one. Learn how to recognise abuse and respond.

🔍 Signs of Abuse in a Child

  • Visible injuries — bruises, cuts, burns — especially repeated
  • Appears malnourished, dirty, or dressed inappropriately for weather
  • Frequently absent from school without explanation
  • Seems fearful, withdrawn, or unusually aggressive
  • Shows inappropriate sexual knowledge or behaviour for age

🏠 Signs of Concern in a Home

  • Children seen begging or working instead of attending school
  • Child frequently left alone or unsupervised for long periods
  • Sounds of crying, screaming, or distress coming from the home
  • Adults who seem hostile about others interacting with the child

✅ What You Should Do

  • Do not confront the abuser — this can endanger the child
  • Report to CPFSA: 1-888-CHILDREN
  • If the child is in immediate danger, call 119
  • You do not need to give your name to report
  • You cannot be punished for a good-faith report

🚨 Trafficking Can Happen in Plain Sight in Your Community

Traffickers operate in communities, schools, and online. Recognising the signs early and acting quickly can save a child's life.

🔴 Community Warning Signs

  • Young girls seen regularly with older men giving them gifts
  • Children who appear coached or scripted when speaking
  • Unfamiliar adults acting as "guardians" for neighbourhood children
  • Children working in homes or businesses without attending school
  • Children appearing disoriented, fearful, or not allowed to speak freely

📱 Online Recruitment Signs

  • Young girls with sudden unexplained brand items or phones
  • Children speaking of "parties" with older adults they barely know
  • Sudden unexplained disappearances and reappearances
  • Children sharing adult-type content on social media

📞 Report Trafficking

  • NATFATIP Hotline: 876-922-2371
  • JCF Counter-Trafficking Unit: 876-922-2371
  • Or call 1-888-CHILDREN
  • You can report anonymously — your name will be protected

🤝 How Neighbours Can Help

  • Be someone a child in your community can safely talk to
  • Offer practical support to overwhelmed parents (food, childcare)
  • Involve churches, community groups and schools in child safety
  • Organise community safety watch programmes
  • Check in on families you can see are under visible stress

⛪ The Role of Faith Communities

  • Host parenting workshops with trained facilitators
  • Provide structured mentorship for at-risk youth
  • Create safe, supervised after-school spaces for children
  • Partner with CPFSA and social workers for community outreach

🏫 The Role of Schools

  • Mandatory reporting of all suspected abuse — no exceptions
  • Guidance counsellors who are visible and accessible to students
  • Peer support and structured anti-bullying programmes
  • Anonymous reporting mechanisms for students to use safely

⚖️ Mandatory Reporting — Know Your Legal Duty

As a teacher or school professional, you are a mandated reporter under Jamaica's Child Care and Protection Act. Failure to report suspected abuse is a criminal offence. You only need to report — not investigate.

🏫 Signs to Watch For at School

  • Child makes a direct or indirect disclosure of abuse
  • Unexplained injuries, chronic hunger, or fatigue
  • Sudden significant grade drops or increased absences
  • Child appears afraid to go home after school
  • Age-inappropriate sexual drawings, language, or behaviour

📋 Steps for Teachers Who Suspect Abuse

  • Document the observation with date, time and full detail
  • Report immediately to your school principal
  • The school must report to CPFSA within 24 hours
  • Do NOT investigate or confront the alleged abuser
  • Continue to monitor and provide emotional support

📚 Free Training Is Available

The Ministry of Education and CPFSA offer free child safeguarding training for Jamaican schools and educators. Contact moey.gov.jm or cpfsa.gov.jm to schedule a session for your school today.
